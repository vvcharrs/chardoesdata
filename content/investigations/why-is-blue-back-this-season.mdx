## Introduction

Blue has returned with the particular confidence of a colour that can be read as classic, corporate, sentimental, pragmatic, or rebellious depending on the cut and context. This placeholder investigation sets up the eventual reporting structure for a full data story.

<PullQuote credit="Working thesis">
  Colour trends do not simply arrive; they are repeated into visibility by
  designers, retailers, platforms, and consumers.
</PullQuote>

## Question

What evidence would show that blue is genuinely back this season, rather than simply present in the usual background noise of fashion imagery and retail assortments?

The finished investigation should define what "back" means before measuring it. Possible definitions include a year-over-year increase in runway looks, a higher share of new retail arrivals, rising search interest, or increased usage in editorial captions and social posts.

## Hypothesis

The working hypothesis is that blue is not returning as a single colour story. Instead, different blues may be doing different cultural work: pale blue as softness, navy as restraint, cobalt as visibility, and denim blue as everyday utility.

The full version could combine runway image tagging, retailer product feeds, Google Trends, search platform data, social captions, and fashion media archives. Each source should include a collection window, a sampling frame, and known limitations.

## Methodology

The methodology should separate colour detection from interpretation. Automated colour extraction can identify dominant hues in images, but human review is still needed for ambiguous garments, lighting shifts, denim, prints, and styling context.

## Limitations

Colour analysis is sensitive to lighting, photography, skin tone contrast, garment material, image compression, and subjective naming conventions. Retail metadata may also reflect merchandising language rather than visual colour.

The finished piece should state where the sample overrepresents certain markets, designers, retailers, price points, body types, or platforms.
